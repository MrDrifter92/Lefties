Drying Time: Let It Breathe

Your carpet might feel damp to the touch for a few hours. Drying time can vary from 4-12 hours depending on the fibre, airflow, and the humidity here in the Bay.

  • ✓ Go easy on it: Avoid walking on the carpet with shoes. If you must walk on it, use clean socks or bare feet to prevent re-soiling.
  • ✓ Get the air moving: Open up some windows or turn on a fan or air-conditioning to speed things up. Good ventilation is key.

Moving Furniture Back

It's best to wait at least 24 hours before moving heavy furniture back into place. This ensures the carpet underneath is completely dry and prevents furniture polish or wood stains from transferring to the damp fibres. We may have placed protective tabs or blocks under legs—leave them there until everything is bone dry.

What About Spills?

Accidents happen. If you have a fresh spill:

  • ✓ Don't panic. Don't rub. Rubbing pushes the stain deeper into the fibres.
  • ✓ Blot, blot, blot. Grab a clean, white, dry towel and blot the spill, working from the outside in. Absorb as much as you can.
  • ✓ Then call Lefties. For anything tricky, give us a call before you reach for a supermarket cleaner. We can give you the right advice.

If a Spot Reappears...

Occasionally, a stubborn spot can wick back up to the surface as the carpet dries. If this happens within a couple of days, let us know.
